## Data Privacy

- **_Data privacy_**, also known as <u>**_"information privacy"_**</u>, refers to the rights and expectations of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, and shared
- Ensuring data privacy helps maintain trust between individuals and organization, supports compliance with legal regulations, and upholds ethical standards in data handling

## Importance of Data Privacy

- Protects Personal Information

  - Persoanl data includes sensitive information such as <u>**_names_**</u>, <u>**_addresses_**</u>, <u>**_financial details_**</u>, <u>**_medical records_**</u>, and <u>**_online activities_**</u>
  - **Examples**\
 A data breach at bank can expose customer financial information leading to <u>**_fraudulent transactions_**</u>

## Ensures Compliance with Laws and Regulations

- Governments and regulatory bodies inforce data privacy laws to protect individuals' rights
- Organizations must comply with laws like:

  - <u>**_GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)_**</u> - EU law protecting user data.
  - <u>**_CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)_**</u> - Protects California residents' personal information
  - <u>**_HIPPA (Health Insurance Protability and Accountability)_**</u> - Safegaurds medical records in the U.S.

## Builds Trust Between Business and Consumers

- Prioritize data privacy to gain customer trust and loyalty
- Customers are more likey to do businesss with companies that respect their privacy and handle data responsibly
- Example:\
A social media platform that transparently explains how user data is used will attract more trust compared to one involved in data scandals

## Prevents Data Breaches and Cyberattacks

- Strong <u>data privacy polices</u> help protect agains <u>**_hacking_**</u>, <u>**_phising_**</u>, and <u>**_ransomware_**</u>
- <u>**Cybercriminals**</u> exploit weak data protetction to steal and sell personal information on the dark web
- Example:\
In 2021, a major data breach at Facebook exposed <u>**_533 million users_**</u> personal data, which was later found on hacker forums

## Protects Freedom and Prevents Discrimination

- Miuse of personal data can lead to discrimination based on <u>**_race_**</u>, <u>**_gender_**</u>, <u>**_health status_**</u>, or <u>**_financial history_**</u>
- Data privacy helps prevent <u>**_mass surveillance_**</u>, <u>**_government overreach_**</u>, and <u>**_unauthorized tracking of individuals_**</u>
- Examples:\
Employers using AI-powered hiring system must ensure personal data isn't used unfairly in hiring decisions

# Data Security

Data security refers to the <u>**_techinical measures_**</u> and <u>**_practices_**</u> used to protect data from <u>**_unauthorized access_**</u>, <u>**_breaches_**</u>, <u>**_theft_**</u>, or <u>**_corruption_**</u>. It ensures that sensitive information remains <u>**_condidential_**</u>, <u>**_intact_**</u>, and <u>**_available only to authorized indivuals_**</u>

## Types of Data Security

- <u>**_Encryption_**</u>\
&emsp;Is the use of algorithms to scramble data and hide its true meaning
- <u>**_Data Erasure_**</u>\
&emsp;Is <u>**_the process of securely deleting data_**</u> so that can cannot be recoverd
- <u>**_Data Masking_**</u>\
&emsp;Is a technique used to <u>**_hide real data_**</u> by replacing it with fictitous but realistic data
- <u>**_Data Resiliency_**</u>\
&emsp;Refers to <u>**_the ability of a system to recover quickly and maintain availability_**</u> after a disruption, cuperattack, or hardware failure

# Data Protection

Data protection refers to set of <u>**_pratices_**</u>, <u>**_policies_**</u> and <u>**_technologies_**</u> aimed at safeguarding data from <u>**_loss_**</u>, <u>**_corruption_**</u>, <u>**_theft_**</u>, or <u>**_unauthorized access_**</u>. It ensures that data remains secure, accurate, and accessible while upholding privacy standards

# Fundamental Privacy Principles

Serve as the foundation for data protection laws and organizational policies to ensure individuals' personal data is handled responsibly

## Privacy Principles

- <u>**_Transparency_**</u>
- <u>**_Purpose Minimization_**</u>
- <u>**_Accuracy_**</u>
- <u>**_Storage Limitation_**</u>
- <u>**_Integrity and Confidentiality(Security)_**</u>
- <u>**_Accountability_**</u>

### Transparency

- Organization must inform individuals about how their data is <u>**_collected_**</u>, <u>**_used_**</u>, <u>**_shared_**</u>, and <u>**_stored_**</u>
- Example:\
&emsp;Privacy polocies on website detailing data practices

### Purpose Limitation

- Personel data should only be collected for <u>**_specific_**</u>, <u>**_clear_**</u>, and <u>**_lagitimate purposes_**</u>
- Examples:\
&emsp;A company should not use customer data for advertising if it was collected for accound verification

### Data Minimization

- Organization should only collect the minimum amound of personal data necessary for a specific purpose
- Example:\
&emsp;A mobile app requiring only an email, not a full address, for account creation

### Accuracy

- Personal data must be kept <u>**_accurate_**</u>, <u>**_up-to-date_**</u>, and <u>**_corrected_**</u> when necessary
- Example:\
&emsp;Banks allowing customers to update personal details to prevent errors

### Storage Limitation

- Personal data should not be retained longer than necessary for its intended purpose
- Example:\
&emsp;A business deleting inactive customer accounts after a set period

### Integrity and Confidentiality (Security)

- Oragnization must protect data against <u>**_unauthorized access_**</u>, <u>**_breaching_**</u>, or <u>**_loss using strong security measures_**</u>
- Examples:\
&emsp;Encryption, firewalls, and access controls for sensitive financial data

### Accountability

- Companies and Organizations must be responsible for complying with privacy laws and demontrating their adherence
- Examples:\
&emsp;Appointing a <u>**_Data Protection Officer (DPO)_**</u> to oversee complaince with privacy laws

# Legal and Regulatory Frameworks

- <u>**_General Data Protection Regultory (GDPR)_**</u> - European Union
- <u>**_California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)_**</u> - United States (California)
- <u>**_Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act 101073)_**</u>


## General Data Protection Regulatoin (GDOR) - European Union

- Is the toughest privacy and security law in the world
- It imposes obligation onto organizations anywhere, so long as they target or collect data related to people in the EU
- The regulation was put into effect on <u>**_May 25, 2018_**</u>
- The GDPR will levy harsh fines against those who violate its privacy and security standards, with penalties reaching into the <u>**_tens of millions of euros_**</u>

### Key Principles of GDPR

- Requires clear user consent before collecting data (like name, email address, location information, ethnicity, gender, biometric data, web cookies)
- Grants users the <u>**_"Right to be Forgotten"_**</u> (deleting their data upon request)
- Data breaches notification must be sent within 72 hours
- Enforces strict penalties for violations <u>**_(up to €20 million or 4% of annual revenue)_**</u>
&emsp;Meta(Facebook) was fined <u>**_€1.2 billion_**</u> for transferring EU user data to the U.S. without proper safeguards

## California Consumer Privacy Act(CCOA) - United States (California)

- The California Privacy Act of 2018 (CCPA) gives consumers more control over the personal information that business collect about them

### Privacy Rights for Consumers

- The right to know what data is collected and how it is used
- The right to <u>**_op-out of data sales_**</u> or <u>**_sharing of their personal information_**</u> 
- The right to request deletion of personal information
- The right to non-discrimination for exercising their CCPA rights
- On <u>**_January 1, 2023_**</u>, the CCPA added an additional privacy protection, granting new consumer rights to privacy

  - THe right to correct inaccurate personal information that a business has about them
  - The right to limit the use of disclosure of sensitive personal information collected about them
  
- Example Violation:\
&emsp;Sephore was fined <u>**_€1.2 million in 2022_**</u> failing to disclose data tracking and not allowing customers to opt-out of data sales

## Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act 10173) - Philippines

- Is a Philippine law designed to protect the privacy of individuals by regulating how personal data is <u>**_collected_**</u>, <u>**_stored_**</u>, and <u>**_processed_**</u> by both government and private entities, ensuring the secure handling of personal information within information and communicate systems across the country
- Establishes the <u>**_National Privacy Commision (NPC)_**</u> to oversee its implimentation

  ### <u>**_National Privacy Commision (NPC)_**</u>

  - The <u>**_National Privacy Commision_**</u>, or <u>**_NPC_**</u>, is an independent government body in the Philippines created under <u>**_Rebuplic Act No. 10173_**</u> or the <u>**_Data Privacy Act of 2012_**</u>
  - The Commission is mandated to administer and implement the provision of the Act and to monitor and ensure compliance of the country with international standards set for data protection

- Applies to any organization processing peronal data of Philippin citizen
- Ket Features:

  - Requires <u>**_Data Protection Officers (DPO's)_**</u> in organization
  - Consumers have the right to <u>**_access_**</u>, <u>**_correct_**</u>, and <u>**_delete_**</u> their personal data
  - Imposes penalties on businesses that misuse or fail to protect personal data

- Example Violation:\
&emsp;<u>**_COMELEC Data Breach (2016)_**</u> - <u>**_The Philippine Commission on Elections_**</u> suffered a massive voter data leak, exposing <u>**_55 million records_**</u>

## Why Are These Laws Important?

- Protects consumer rights and ensures data is handled responsibly
- Ecnourages companies to implement stronger security measures
- Reduces <u>**_identiy theft_**</u>, <u>**_fraud_**</u>, and <u>**_unauthorized data use_**</u>, holds organization accountable for data misuse
